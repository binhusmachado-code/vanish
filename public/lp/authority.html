The Data Broker Problem

The data broker industry generates $197 billion annually — from selling your information.

Your home address, phone number, salary range, court records, and relatives' names are available for purchase on over 484 commercial databases — most of which you have never heard of and did not consent to.

Data brokers acquire personal information from public records, social media, purchase histories, and third-party aggregators. They package and resell this data to marketers, employers, insurers, and anyone willing to pay — including bad actors.

The legal framework governing data brokers remains fragmented. While CCPA and GDPR provide some remedies, exercising opt-out rights across hundreds of brokers requires sustained, technical, and legally precise effort that most individuals lack the time or expertise to execute.

For executives, journalists, attorneys, and public figures, the risk is not hypothetical. The consequences of exposed personal data range from targeted harassment to physical security threats.

The data broker industry operates largely without public awareness or meaningful regulation. Most professionals discover their exposure only after an incident occurs.

The VANISH Process

A systematic, legally compliant approach to eliminating your data from commercial databases.

I.
Full Exposure Audit

We conduct a comprehensive audit of your data exposure across all 484 brokers in our database, generating a detailed report of every instance of your personal information currently available for purchase.

II.
Opt-Out & Deletion

We submit legally compliant opt-out and deletion requests on your behalf, using the precise formats and verification procedures each broker requires. Our team manages all correspondence.

III.
Verification & Monitoring

We verify each removal with documented confirmation, then monitor all 484 databases quarterly. When brokers re-list your data — and they will attempt to — we remove it again automatically.
